Get started

Getting started checklist

Step-by-step guide to Software Trust Manager. Follow these steps to start signing or see our Get started guide ↗

0%

Download the DigiCert ONE Client for your system.

The Unified Client Manager handles your connection and credentials securely. Select your operating system to download the correct version.

  1. 1 Install the app: Download and run the setup wizard. Review the Master Services Agreement, choose install options, then finish and launch.
  2. 2 Authenticate to host environment: By default, DigiCert ONE Clients connect to the US Production environment. If your account is in a different region, follow our Switch DigiCert ONE Environment guide to update your configuration.
  3. 3 Sync your user profile: Open DigiCert ONE Clients, and then sign in with your credentials.

Install the DigiCert ONE Command Line Tool.

The CLI tool lets you automate signing operations in your build pipeline.

  1. 1 Download the CLI installer for your operating system from the DigiCert ONE portal.
  2. 2 Run the installer and follow the prompts to complete the setup.
  3. 3 Verify installation by running smctl version in your terminal.

Verify you are ready to sign.

Before signing, confirm your environment and credentials are correctly configured.

  1. 1 Confirm your certificate is issued and in an active state in DigiCert ONE.
  2. 2 Check your key pair is accessible and the HSM or keypair profile is linked to your account.
  3. 3 Test connectivity by running smctl healthcheck to ensure your environment is reachable.

Sign your first artifact.

You're ready to sign. Use the CLI or your CI/CD integration to sign your first build artifact.

  1. 1 Choose an artifact — an executable, package, or container image you want to sign.
  2. 2 Run the signing command: smctl sign --keypair-alias <alias> --input <file>
  3. 3 Verify the signature and check that it appears in your Signature logs in STM.
